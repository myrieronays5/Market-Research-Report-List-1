<p><p>The hydrogen gas onsite generator market features two primary types: traditional alkaline electrolyzers and PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane) electrolyzers. Traditional alkaline electrolyzers utilize a liquid alkaline solution to conduct electricity, making them suitable for large-scale applications due to their cost-effectiveness. In contrast, PEM electrolyzers use a solid polymer membrane, allowing for higher efficiency and quick response to variable power sources, making them ideal for smaller, more dynamic systems. Both technologies cater to the growing demand for clean hydrogen production.</p></p>
