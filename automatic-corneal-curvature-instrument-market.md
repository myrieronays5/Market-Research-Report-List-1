<p><p>The Automatic Corneal Curvature Instrument market includes various types such as the Helmholtz Type and the Javal-Schiotz Type. The Helmholtz Type utilizes a dual-light source to measure curvature changes, providing precise topographic data for refractive assessments. In contrast, the Javal-Schiotz Type employs a combination of optical principles and mechanical movements to gauge corneal curvature, often allowing for easier portability and manual operation. Both instruments are integral in ophthalmology for diagnosing and monitoring corneal conditions.</p></p>
