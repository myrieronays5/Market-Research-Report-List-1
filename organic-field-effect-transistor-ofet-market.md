<p><p>Organic Field-effect Transistors (OFETs) are a type of transistor that utilize organic semiconductor materials to control electrical currents. They come in two primary types: N-type and P-type. N-type transistors use electrons as the charge carriers, making them suitable for applications requiring electron mobility, such as displays and sensors. In contrast, P-type transistors rely on holes as charge carriers, offering benefits in complementary circuits. The market for both types is expanding in flexible electronics, organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs), and renewable energy applications.</p></p>
