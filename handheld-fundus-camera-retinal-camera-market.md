<p><p>The handheld fundus camera market is divided into two primary types: mydriatic and non-mydriatic cameras. Mydriatic cameras require the use of dilating eye drops to expand the pupils, allowing for a comprehensive view of the retina. In contrast, non-mydriatic cameras do not necessitate pupil dilation, making them more convenient for quick and comfortable examinations. Both types serve critical roles in retinal assessments, with non-mydriatic options being particularly beneficial for screening in diverse settings.</p></p>
